Algebras in Field Theory and Gravity: An Overview

Edward Witten

About the speaker

Edward Witten is a theoretical physicist and mathematician who is known for his work in string theory and quantum field theory. He is a professor at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey, and has made significant contributions to the fields of theoretical physics and mathematics. Witten is a recipient of numerous awards, including the Fields Medal and the Fundamental Physics Prize. His work has had a profound impact on the development of modern physics and has inspired generations of researchers.

Abstract


I will discuss general properties of the algebra of the observables in a region or accessible to an observer in ordinary quantum field theory and in the presence of gravity.
